COMMON PROBLEMS AND OUR SOLUTIONS

In general, most persons experience social isolation and loneliness at various stages of their lives. However, there are also more specific problems to address:

PATIENT ISOLATION:
Patients have limited to no interaction with persons outside of the hospital, hospital volunteers are entirely domestic persons (also frequently disallowed due to health precautions), and lack of social skills leave survivors feeling isolated even after discharge.

GENERAL SOCIAL LONELINESS AND CULURAL TABOO:
This is a common feeling among ALL persons (regardless of one's general health). Social loneliness and isolation have also been known to contribute to depression and suicide.

OVERPOLLUTED ONLINE MEDIUMS:
An overpollution of penpaling, and dating sites with an overflux of random users, lack of safety in private information exchange, unclear intentions among users, and the consequence of quantity over quality due to unlimited amount of "matches".

LACK OF OPTIONS:
In most cases, the only form to meet/help disadvantaged beings (for both altruistic and therapeutic reasons) is through in-person volunteering at select hospitals and shelters.


We are fighting the stigma of shame that is associated with feeling lonely, and isolated by creating a medium that ALL persons can shamelessly access, share, and interact with others in a fun, engaging manner.

MYRIAD OF OPTIONS:
Patients can choose to match with fellow patients penpals, and/or general non-patient penpals.

ENCOURAGEMENT OF DIVERSITY:
Open to all ages, genders, ethnicities, professions, income levels, etc.


ELIMINATING THE STIGMA:
Unlike the social taboos that surround having a therapist, etc - sharing your penpal experience is an unique, shameless journey.



QUALITY OVER QUANTITY:
General penpals may only form a maximum of 2 penpairs at a time so one can focus on nurturing their penpair relationship. However, you may increase your number of penpairs gradually over time.

A SAFE HAVEN:
Verified signup process, privatization of users' contact information, and heavy monitorization of personal information exchanges.
